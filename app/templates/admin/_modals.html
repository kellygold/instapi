InstaPi Guide

📷 Adding Photos

Upload from phone: Tap "Upload Photos" to pick images from your camera roll. Works from any device — iPhone, Android, or computer.
Share with family: Tap "Copy Link" to get a shareable upload link. Anyone with the link can add photos — no app or account needed.
Google Photos: Optional. Select photos from your Google Photos library. Requires signing in with Google each time.

🖼️ Photo Gallery

View all photos on your frame. Tap any photo to see it full size. Tap the × to delete it. Use the Small/Large buttons to change the grid size. If multiple people have uploaded photos, use the filter chips to view by uploader.
On USB mode, remember to hit "Sync Frame" after making changes.

☁️ Family Sync

Personal frame: If it's just your frame, you don't need sync at all — just upload photos directly.
Host frame (master): Set this as the primary frame. Add child frames and share tokens with family. All uploads go here, and child frames pull from it automatically.
Child frame: Syncs photos from the host frame every 30 minutes (configurable). A status card shows connection state, photo count, and next sync time. You can also tap "Sync Now" to pull immediately.

🔄 Sync Frame (USB mode)

When you add or delete photos, the USB frame doesn't update immediately. An orange "Sync Frame" button appears — tap it to push your changes. The frame will briefly disconnect and reconnect.

🎬 Slideshow Settings

Slide Duration: How long each photo is shown (3–60 seconds).
Transition: How photos change — fade, slide, or zoom.
Shuffle: Random order instead of sequential.
Ken Burns: A gentle slow pan and zoom effect on each photo.

In HDMI mode, these control the actual slideshow. In USB mode, they only affect the browser preview — your frame uses its own built-in slideshow.

⚙️ System

Update & Restart: Pulls the latest code from GitHub and restarts the app. This is how your frame gets over-the-air updates — no SSH needed.
Switch Mode: Toggle between USB and HDMI display modes. Requires a restart to take full effect.
Factory Reset: Deletes all photos and resets all settings to defaults. Returns the frame to the setup screen. This cannot be undone.
